Songhai Empire

The Songhai Empire (c. 1460–1591) was a major West African power centered at Gao, thriving on Trans-Saharan trade in gold and salt.

It succeeded the Mali Empire and reached its height under rulers such as Sonni Ali and Askia Mohammed the Great.

Geography

The Songhai Empire controlled the Niger River region in West Africa, allowing strong trade and communication across the region.

Kings and Leaders

Sonni Ali the Great

Sonni Ali was a powerful military leader who expanded the Songhai Empire by conquering Timbuktu and Djenné.

Askia Mohammed the Great

Askia Mohammed strengthened the empire through Islamic governance, centralized administration, and support for education.

Decline of the Empire

Internal conflicts and the Moroccan invasion of 1591 led to the collapse of the Songhai Empire and its fragmentation into smaller states.
